🆘 EMERGENCY RESOURCES

Get Help Right Now

Every second matters. Find emergency contacts and step-by-step guidance for your country below.

🚨 In Immediate Danger?
Call your local emergency number immediately. If it's safe to do so, get out of the situation first. Your life matters above everything else.
📞 Call 911 (US) 📞 Call 112 (EU) 📞 Call 100 (India) 📞 Call 999 (UK)
📋 What To Do In An Emergency Situation
01
Get to Safety First
If possible, leave the location. Go to a neighbor, public place, or trusted friend. Don't stop to collect belongings — your safety is the priority.
02
Call Emergency Services
Dial your local emergency number (911, 999, 112, 100, etc.). If you can't speak, stay on the line — operators are trained to help silent callers.
03
Contact a Trusted Person
Reach out to a trusted friend, family member, or neighbor who can help you. Have a code word pre-arranged so they know to call for help without asking questions.
04
Seek Medical Attention
Even if injuries seem minor, get evaluated at a hospital or clinic. Medical records may be important for any legal proceedings later.
05
Preserve Evidence
If safe, document injuries with photos. Save threatening messages or emails. Keep a written record with dates and descriptions for police or legal use.
06
Contact a DV Hotline
Domestic violence hotlines offer free, confidential support 24/7. They can help with safety planning, legal options, shelter, and counseling referrals.
07
Create a Safety Plan
Work with a counselor to prepare for future incidents: identify safe exits, pack an emergency bag, arrange where to go, and who to contact.
08
Know Your Legal Rights
You can file for a restraining order or protection order in most countries. Contact a legal aid society or DV organization to understand your options.
